Avant specializes in serving borrowers with fair credit scores between 580-669. How to Choose the Right Personal Loan

Consider Your Credit Score

Your credit score is the single biggest factor determining your interest rate. Before applying, check your score for free through services like Credit Karma or directly from your credit card issuer. This helps you target lenders most likely to approve you at favorable rates.

  • Excellent Credit (720+): You'll qualify for the best rates (under 12% APR). Consider LightStream or SoFi first.
  • Good Credit (670-719): Most mainstream lenders will approve you. Compare offers from SoFi, Discover, and Marcus.
  • Fair Credit (580-669): Focus on Avant, Upstart, or credit unions that may be more flexible.
  • Poor Credit (below 580): Consider Upstart or secured personal loans. Be cautious of predatory lenders.

Compare Total Loan Costs

Don't focus solely on monthly payments—calculate the total cost of each loan by multiplying your monthly payment by the number of payments. A lower monthly payment spread over more months often costs significantly more in total interest.

Watch for Origination Fees

Some lenders charge origination fees ranging from 1% to 10% of the loan amount. These are typically deducted from your loan proceeds, meaning a $10,000 loan with a 5% origination fee only puts $9,500 in your pocket while you repay the full $10,000 plus interest.

Pro Tip: Use each lender's prequalification tool to check your estimated rate with just a soft credit pull. This lets you compare multiple offers without affecting your credit score. Only proceed with a formal application once you've found the best option.

Personal Loan Use Cases & Considerations

Debt Consolidation

Personal loans are excellent for consolidating high-interest credit card debt. The average credit card APR exceeds 21% in 2026, while qualified borrowers can get personal loans at half that rate. Consolidating also simplifies your finances by replacing multiple payments with one fixed monthly amount.

Home Improvement

Unlike home equity loans, personal loans don't use your home as collateral, making them a safer choice for renovations. Some lenders like LightStream offer lower rates specifically for home improvement purposes when you can document the project.

Medical Expenses

Before using a personal loan for medical bills, always negotiate with providers first—many offer payment plans or financial assistance programs. If you still need financing, a personal loan typically offers better rates than medical credit cards like CareCredit.

Emergency Expenses

For true emergencies, the fast funding from online lenders like LightStream or SoFi (often same-day or next-day) can provide critical relief. However, building an emergency fund remains the better long-term strategy.

What to Avoid When Getting a Personal Loan

  • Payday loans: These predatory products charge APRs of 400%+ and trap borrowers in debt cycles.
  • Borrowing more than needed: Only borrow what you'll actually use—you pay interest on every dollar.
  • Ignoring the fine print: Read all terms carefully, especially regarding prepayment penalties and late fees.
  • Skipping prequalification: Multiple hard credit pulls can hurt your score; prequalify first.
  • Choosing based on monthly payment alone: Longer terms mean lower payments but more total interest.

Frequently Asked Questions

What credit score do I need for a personal loan?

Most traditional lenders require a minimum credit score of 580-670 for personal loans. However, some online lenders work with borrowers who have scores as low as 550. The best rates (under 10% APR) typically require scores of 720 or higher.

How fast can I get money from a personal loan?

Many online lenders offer same-day or next-business-day funding after approval. Traditional banks may take 3-7 business days. Some lenders like LightStream and SoFi can fund loans within 24 hours of approval for qualified borrowers.

What can I use a personal loan for?

Personal loans can be used for almost any legal purpose including debt consolidation, home improvement, medical expenses, wedding costs, emergency expenses, moving costs, and major purchases. Some lenders restrict use for business purposes, gambling, or education.

Are personal loans better than credit cards for large purchases?

Personal loans typically offer lower APRs (6-36%) compared to credit cards (15-29%), making them better for large purchases you'll pay off over time. Fixed monthly payments also make budgeting easier. However, credit cards with 0% intro APR offers may be better for amounts you can pay off within 12-21 months.

Does applying for a personal loan hurt my credit score?

Most lenders offer prequalification with a soft credit check that doesn't affect your score. The formal application involves a hard inquiry, which typically drops your score by 5-10 points temporarily. However, successfully managing the loan can improve your credit mix and payment history over time.

The Bottom Line

Personal loans offer a powerful tool for consolidating debt, financing major expenses, or handling emergencies—often at significantly lower rates than credit cards. The key to getting the best deal is shopping around, comparing multiple prequalified offers, and understanding the true total cost including any fees.

For most borrowers with good credit, SoFi's combination of competitive rates, zero fees, and unique benefits like unemployment protection makes it our top recommendation. Those with excellent credit should also check LightStream's rates, while borrowers with fair credit or limited history may find better approval odds with Upstart or Avant.

Whatever your situation, take time to compare at least 3-5 lenders before committing. The difference between a 10% APR and 15% APR on a $15,000 loan over 5 years amounts to nearly $2,000 in extra interest—well worth the effort of shopping around.
