    Auto Insurance Guide

    Auto insurance is required in nearly every state. Understanding the different types of coverage helps you choose the right protection without overpaying.

    Types of Auto Coverage

    Liability insurance is required in 49 of 50 states. It covers damage you cause to others. Collision coverage pays to repair your vehicle after an accident. Comprehensive coverage protects against theft, vandalism, hail, and more. Uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage protects you when the at-fault driver lacks adequate insurance.

    Tips to Lower Your Auto Insurance Rates

    Bundle your auto and homeowners insurance for a 10-25% multi-policy discount. Increase your deductible to save 15-30%. Compare quotes from at least three insurers every year.

    Frequently Asked Questions

    Most adults need auto insurance (if they own a vehicle), health insurance, and renters or homeowners insurance. If you have dependents, life insurance is essential.
    A deductible is the amount you pay out-of-pocket before your insurance kicks in. Higher deductibles mean lower monthly premiums.

    Insurance Glossary

    Premium

    The amount you pay for your insurance policy, typically monthly, quarterly, or annually.

    Deductible

    The out-of-pocket amount you must pay before your insurance begins covering costs.

    Coinsurance

    The percentage of costs you share with your insurer after meeting your deductible.
