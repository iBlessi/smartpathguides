Crypto Staking Guide 2026: Earn Passive Income

Crypto staking lets you put your digital assets to work, earning yield simply by holding and locking them in a network. In 2026, staking has become one of the most widely used strategies for generating passive income from crypto — but not all staking is created equal. This guide covers everything: what staking is, the best coins, APY comparisons, liquid staking protocols, risks, and tax treatment.

Last updated: March 2026  ·  17 min read

What Is Crypto Staking?

Staking is the process of locking up (committing) your cryptocurrency to support the operation of a proof-of-stake (PoS) blockchain network. In exchange for providing this service, stakers receive rewards in the form of additional tokens.

To understand staking, it helps to contrast it with proof-of-work (PoW), the consensus mechanism used by Bitcoin. In PoW, miners compete to solve computational puzzles, consuming large amounts of energy. In PoS, validators are chosen to propose and attest to blocks based on how much of the native token they've staked as collateral.

Ethereum's transition to PoS ("The Merge") in September 2022 was the watershed moment that brought staking to mainstream attention. Today, more than $50 billion in ETH is staked, and dozens of major blockchains operate on PoS or its variants.

Key point: When you stake, you're providing a service to the network. Your rewards come from new token issuance (inflation) and/or transaction fees, depending on the network.

Top Staking Coins: In-Depth Review

ETH Ethereum

Ethereum is the second-largest cryptocurrency by market cap and the most widely staked asset by total value locked. Native ETH staking requires 32 ETH to run a full validator node. However, staking pools and liquid staking protocols have eliminated the minimum for most users.

Ethereum's staking rewards come from two sources: consensus layer rewards (new ETH issuance) and execution layer tips (priority fees). The total effective yield hovers between 3–4.5% depending on network activity.

SOL Solana

Solana offers one of the most accessible staking experiences. With no minimum requirement and epoch-based unbonding of just 2–3 days, Solana staking is ideal for users who want high yields with relatively short lock-ups.

Solana liquid staking through Marinade Finance (mSOL) or Jito (jitoSOL) provides liquid representations of staked SOL usable in Solana DeFi.

ADA Cardano

Cardano's staking model is unique: ADA never actually leaves your wallet. Instead of locking funds with a validator, you "delegate" your wallet's staking rights to a stake pool while retaining full custody. Rewards are approximately 4–5% annually and are distributed every epoch (5 days).

DOT Polkadot

Polkadot offers some of the highest staking yields of any established PoS network, currently around 12–14% annually. However, this comes with a significant caveat: a 28-day unbonding period. Once you initiate unstaking, your DOT is inaccessible for nearly a month.

ATOM Cosmos

Cosmos Hub (ATOM) offers the highest nominal staking yield on this list, currently around 15–19%. Cosmos uses a governance-driven inflation model. The 21-day unbonding period is a significant constraint to be aware of.

Liquid Staking: Lido and Rocket Pool

Liquid staking is one of the most significant innovations in the PoS ecosystem. Traditional staking locks up your tokens, preventing you from using them while they're earning rewards. Liquid staking protocols issue you a derivative token representing your staked position.

Lido Finance

Lido is the dominant liquid staking protocol, controlling over 30% of all staked ETH. When you deposit ETH into Lido, you receive stETH (staked ETH) at a 1:1 ratio. Your stETH balance automatically increases each day as staking rewards accrue. You can use stETH across major DeFi protocols (Aave, Curve, Balancer) to earn additional yield.

Rocket Pool

Rocket Pool is a decentralized alternative to Lido. It allows anyone to run a minipool (requires 8 ETH + 2.4 ETH in RPL as collateral) and receive rETH as the liquid staking token. Rocket Pool is more decentralized than Lido (hundreds of node operators) and appeals to users who prioritize network health.

Concentration risk: Lido's dominance in ETH staking is a genuine systemic risk to Ethereum. Diversifying across liquid staking providers (Rocket Pool, Frax, StakeWise) is prudent for large stakers.

Staking on Exchanges vs. Self-Custody

Most major exchanges now offer staking services: Coinbase, Kraken, Binance US, and Crypto.com all allow you to earn staking rewards directly on the platform. The advantages are simplicity and no minimum requirements. The disadvantages are lower yields (exchanges take a cut) and counterparty risk.

Staking Risks You Must Understand

Market Risk

The most underappreciated risk of staking is that your staked tokens can decline in value during the lock-up period. A 15% annual yield means nothing if the underlying asset drops 50% while you can't sell.

Slashing Risk

Validators can be "slashed" — have a portion of their stake destroyed — for provably malicious behavior (double signing) or extended downtime. When using liquid staking protocols, you share in slashing risk proportionally.

Smart Contract Risk

Liquid staking protocols are governed by smart contracts. Bugs, exploits, or governance attacks on these contracts could result in loss of funds. Protocols like Lido have undergone extensive audits, but no smart contract system is perfectly risk-free.

Regulatory Risk

Regulatory treatment of staking remains an evolving area of law. The SEC has at various points argued that staking-as-a-service constitutes an unregistered securities offering. Be aware that rules may change.

Tax Implications of Staking in the US

The IRS's position, informed by Revenue Ruling 2023-14, is that staking rewards are taxable as ordinary income in the year received, at the fair market value at the time of receipt.

  • Every staking reward distribution is a taxable event, even if you don't sell the tokens.
  • The value of tokens at receipt sets their cost basis for future capital gains calculation.
  • When you later sell staked rewards, capital gains (short-term or long-term) apply on top of income recognition.

Tax tip: Crypto tax software like Koinly, TaxBit, or CoinTracker can automate the calculation of staking income and cost basis.

How to Start Staking: Step-by-Step

1

Choose your asset and method

Select a PoS cryptocurrency and decide whether to stake via exchange (easiest), liquid staking protocol (flexible), or native delegation (maximum yield).

2

Acquire the asset

Purchase the token on a regulated exchange like Coinbase or Kraken.

3

Set up a wallet (for non-exchange staking)

Transfer your tokens to a compatible self-custody wallet. For ETH liquid staking, MetaMask + Ledger is a common setup. For SOL, use Phantom.

4

Stake and confirm

Connect your wallet to the staking protocol (e.g., lido.fi, rocketpool.net) or select a validator through your wallet app. Confirm the staking transaction.

5

Track rewards and tax events

Monitor your rewards and record them for tax purposes. Connect your wallet to a crypto tax tool to automate income tracking.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is crypto staking?
Staking is the process of locking up cryptocurrency to support a proof-of-stake blockchain network, in exchange for earning rewards.
How much can I earn staking crypto in 2026?
Yields vary widely: ETH offers ~3.5%, SOL ~6.5%, ADA ~4%, DOT ~13%, and ATOM ~17% annually. Higher yields typically come with longer lock-up periods.
What is liquid staking?
Liquid staking lets you stake your crypto and receive a liquid token in return (e.g., stETH for staked ETH). This token can be used in DeFi while still earning staking rewards.
Is crypto staking taxable in the US?
Yes. The IRS treats staking rewards as ordinary income in the year received. When you later sell the rewards, capital gains tax also applies.
What are the risks of crypto staking?
Key risks include: market risk (asset price falls during lock-up), slashing risk, lock-up risk, smart contract risk (for liquid staking), and regulatory risk.
Can I unstake my crypto at any time?
It depends on the network: Cardano allows instant delegation changes, Solana ~2–3 days, Ethereum days, Cosmos 21 days, Polkadot 28 days. Liquid staking tokens can be sold on DEXs at any time.
