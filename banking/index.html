Getting More From Your Bank

Most Americans are leaving significant money on the table by keeping their savings in a traditional bank account earning 0.01% APY — the national average for brick-and-mortar savings accounts. High-yield savings accounts offered by online banks and credit unions routinely pay 4–5% APY or more, turning an idle $10,000 balance into an extra $400–$500 per year in interest with essentially no additional effort or risk.

Online banks can offer dramatically higher rates because they don't carry the overhead of physical branches. Your deposits are FDIC-insured up to $250,000 (or NCUA-insured at credit unions), meaning the safety of your money is identical to a traditional bank. The only real trade-off is that you manage your account digitally — a minor inconvenience that most savers find well worth the higher yield.

Beyond savings accounts, the banking landscape has expanded to include a wide range of products designed to minimize fees and maximize returns. Online checking accounts with no monthly fees and ATM fee reimbursements, cash management accounts that combine the features of checking and savings, and money market accounts with tiered interest rates all offer compelling alternatives to the traditional bank account. Understanding your options is the first step to keeping more of your money.

What to Look for in a Savings Account

  • Annual Percentage Yield (APY): The single most important number — higher APY means more interest earned on your balance.
  • Minimum balance requirements: Some accounts require $1,000 or more to earn the advertised rate; others have no minimum at all.
  • Monthly fees: Any monthly maintenance fee will eat into your interest earnings — look for fee-free accounts or accounts that waive fees with a modest minimum balance.
  • Withdrawal limits: Federal rules no longer mandate the old six-withdrawal-per-month limit, but some banks still enforce one — check the terms before opening.
  • Ease of transfer: Fast ACH transfers between your savings and checking accounts make it easier to move money when you need it.
💡 How Much More Can You Earn?

A $25,000 emergency fund sitting in a 0.01% APY traditional savings account earns about $2.50 per year. The same balance in a 4.5% APY high-yield account earns $1,125. The switch takes about 10 minutes online and your money stays just as accessible and FDIC-insured.

Choosing the Right Bank for Your Needs

No single bank is best for every person. If you regularly deposit cash or need in-person service, a credit union or a hybrid bank with a large ATM network may be the right fit. If you're comfortable banking entirely online and want to maximize your savings rate, a dedicated online bank typically offers the highest APYs. Many savers use a two-bank strategy: a local bank or credit union for everyday checking and ATM access, paired with an online high-yield savings account where their emergency fund and savings goals live.

Interest rates change frequently based on Federal Reserve policy, so it's worth checking current rates at least a few times per year. Our guides are updated regularly to reflect current APYs so you always have an accurate picture of where rates stand.
