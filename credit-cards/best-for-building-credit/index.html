The Chime Credit Builder works differently from every other card on this list. There's no security deposit in the traditional sense — instead, you move money from your Chime checking account to a Credit Builder account, and that becomes your spending balance. You can only spend what you've moved there, making it impossible to overspend or accrue debt. The "Safer Credit Building" feature automatically pays your balance in full each month using your Credit Builder funds, ensuring every payment is on time. Best suited for people with very bad or no credit who have been denied for traditional cards.

7 Essential Tips for Building Credit Fast

A credit card is a tool — how you use it determines how quickly your score grows. These habits are what separate people who build excellent credit in 12 months from those who stay stuck for years.

1

Pay every bill on time, every time

Payment history is 35% of your FICO score — the single biggest factor. Set up autopay for at least the minimum payment on every card. A single 30-day late payment can drop a good score by 60–110 points.

2

Keep utilization below 10%

Utilization (your balance ÷ your limit) is 30% of your score. If your limit is $500, keep your balance under $50 for maximum score benefit. Under 30% is acceptable; under 10% is optimal.

3

Don't close your first card

Your oldest account determines the "age of credit history" component of your score. Keep your first card open — even if you stop using it. A small recurring charge (like a $10 streaming subscription) keeps it active.

4

Request a credit limit increase after 6 months

A higher limit lowers your utilization ratio without changing your spending. Most issuers will review your account after 6 months of on-time payments. Call or request online — many will approve without a hard inquiry.

5

Pay your balance before the statement closes

Your reported utilization is what appears on your statement date — not your payment due date. Paying before the statement closes means a $0 balance is reported, dramatically improving your utilization score even if you use the card heavily each month.

6

Space out new applications by 6+ months

Each credit application adds a hard inquiry (-2 to -5 points) and lowers your average account age. Don't apply for multiple cards in rapid succession while building credit — wait 6 months between applications.

7

Graduate and level up

After 12–18 months of responsible use, you should be eligible for standard rewards cards. Don't stay on a secured card forever — once your score reaches 670+, apply for a no-fee rewards card and start earning while continuing to build.
