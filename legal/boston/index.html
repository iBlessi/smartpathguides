Finding a Lawyer in Boston

Whether you need a personal injury attorney, employment lawyer, or criminal defense counsel in Boston, MA, choosing the right legal representation is critical to your case outcome.

How to Find the Right Attorney in Boston

  • Check the MA Bar Association directory for licensed attorneys in Boston
  • Look for lawyers who specialize in your specific legal issue
  • Most personal injury lawyers offer free consultations
  • Ask about fee structures -- many work on contingency (no win, no fee)
  • Read reviews and check disciplinary records through the state bar

Personal Injury Law in Boston

If you've been injured in an accident in Boston, MA, you may be entitled to compensation for medical bills, lost wages, and pain and suffering. MA personal injury law has specific statutes of limitations you must be aware of.

Common personal injury cases in Boston include car accidents, slip and falls, workplace injuries, and medical malpractice. Document everything, seek medical attention immediately, and consult an attorney before speaking with insurance adjusters.

Tenant Rights in Boston

Renters in Boston, MA have specific legal protections. Understanding your rights helps you deal with security deposits, lease disputes, repairs, and potential eviction proceedings.

Key Tenant Rights in Boston, MA

  • Right to a habitable living space with working utilities
  • Protection against illegal eviction and retaliation
  • Security deposit return requirements under MA law
  • Right to privacy -- landlords must provide notice before entry

Employment Law in Boston

Workers in Boston, MA are protected by both federal and state employment laws. These cover minimum wage, overtime, workplace safety, discrimination, and wrongful termination.

If you believe your employer has violated your rights, document the incidents, report to HR, and consult an employment attorney in Boston. Many offer free initial consultations and work on contingency.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I find a free lawyer in Boston?

Many attorneys in Boston offer free initial consultations, especially for personal injury cases. You can also contact MA Legal Aid for free or low-cost legal services if you qualify based on income.

What is the statute of limitations in MA?

Statutes of limitations vary by case type in MA. Personal injury claims typically have a 2-3 year window, while contract disputes may have longer periods. Consult a Boston attorney for your specific situation.

Can I break my lease in Boston?

Lease-breaking laws in MA allow early termination in specific circumstances such as military deployment, domestic violence, or uninhabitable conditions. Review your lease and consult local tenant rights resources.

What should I do after a car accident in Boston?

Call 911, document the scene with photos, exchange information with other parties, seek medical attention, and contact a personal injury attorney in Boston before speaking with insurance companies.
